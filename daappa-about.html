Our story

Built from the ground up for private markets

Early 2000s
daappa Core foundation
daappa begins as a fund accounting and administration platform for private equity and private debt. Early clients include fund administrators and development finance institutions across the UK and Europe.
2010s
Luxembourg and Channel Islands expansion
daappa establishes a local team in Luxembourg and grows its client base across the Channel Islands, building deep expertise in the specific structures and regulatory standards of each jurisdiction.
2020–2023
Studio+ and AI development
daappa develops Studio+, the web-based data and intelligence platform above Core. Extractor AI is built to solve the document processing problem that every private markets firm faces quarterly. DataHub, Analytics, and Portal follow.
2024–2025
NAV Oversight and new products
NAV Oversight launches as a standalone SaaS product targeting the micro-manager segment underserved by enterprise platforms. BRD Group partnership strengthens the daappa platform further.

daappa started with a simple observation: private markets fund accounting is genuinely complex, and the firms that do it -- fund administrators, in-house operations teams, development banks -- deserved software built specifically for that complexity rather than adapted from generic accounting or enterprise tools.

That is still what we do. daappa Core is the fund accounting engine. It handles the things that matter in private markets: partnership accounting, LP servicing, complex valuations, multi-jurisdictional regulatory reporting, and the full deal-to-distribution lifecycle. Over 20 years of development has gone into getting the details right.

Studio+ is the newer product -- the data and intelligence layer above Core. It reflects a second observation: having the fund accounting right is necessary but no longer sufficient. The intelligence layer above it -- document processing, portfolio analytics, investor oversight, and client reporting -- is where the operational burden now sits, and where the competitive differentiation is won.

Our clients are not buying software. They are buying the outcome of better-run operations, more transparent client relationships, and a stronger regulatory posture. We try to build our products with that in mind.

How we work

What we believe about building software for private markets

Three principles that guide everything from product design to how we implement and support clients.

01
Domain depth before feature breadth
Private markets is not a generic use case. The complexity of partnership accounting, the specificity of Luxembourg regulatory requirements, the nuances of ESG data collection -- these require genuine domain expertise, not a configurable generic platform. We go deep on the asset class rather than broad across multiple industries.
02
Control and auditability are not optional
Our clients operate under regulatory oversight. Their data is reviewed by auditors. Their reporting goes to institutional investors. The platform must be auditable by design -- full data lineage, maker-checker workflows, complete audit trails. We don't treat these as features. They are the foundation.
03
Implementation is the start, not the end
A fund accounting or data platform that doesn't get used properly is worse than no platform at all. We scope implementations carefully, run parallel periods, and support clients with the same team that built their configuration. The relationship starts at implementation, not ends there.

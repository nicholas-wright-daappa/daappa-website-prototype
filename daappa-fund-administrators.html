The pressures you're facing

Fund administration is getting harder

01
Clients want more, faster
Investors expect real-time data visibility, faster NAV turnarounds, and digital document exchange. Delivering that on legacy systems means adding headcount rather than adding technology.
02
Mandates are becoming more complex
Hybrid funds, multi-instrument structures, ESG obligations, and SFDR reporting have made every client mandate more complicated than the last. Your system has to handle it without custom development work for each client.
03
Regulatory requirements keep moving
CSSF Circular 24/856, AIFMD II, ILPA reporting standards, ESG/SFDR. Each change requires a system that can adapt without a six-month implementation project.
04
Document processing is a bottleneck
Quarterly portco reporting, LP documents, financial statements. Analysts spend days re-keying data from PDFs when they should be focused on oversight and client service.
05
Competitors are differentiating on technology
Larger administrators are investing in platform technology to win and retain mandates. Boutique firms need to offer comparable capability without enterprise-scale IT budgets.

daappa is built for this operating environment

We have been building fund administration technology for over 20 years. Our clients are boutique and mid-sized administrators who need the depth of an enterprise system and the flexibility to configure it themselves -- without relying on us for every change.

The combination of daappa Core for fund accounting and daappa Studio+ for data, analytics, and client portal gives administrators a complete technology stack. Core handles the back-office rigour. Studio+ handles the client-facing and intelligence layer above it.

Both are built on private markets data models, not adapted from generic accounting or BI tools. That matters when your clients run complex structures and your regulatory obligations are specific to this asset class.

Built to scale your operations without scaling your team

The commercial model for fund administration depends on margin per client and per fund. Technology that requires manual processing for every transaction, document, and report destroys that margin as volumes grow.

daappa's straight-through processing model is designed around multi-client, multi-fund volume. Transaction postings generate GL entries automatically. Reports schedule and distribute automatically. Extractor AI processes incoming documents without analyst intervention.

Common questions

Fund administrators — frequently asked

Does daappa replace our current fund accounting system?
Yes, if you implement daappa Core. Core is a full replacement for your existing fund accounting system -- not an overlay. The implementation process includes data migration from your current system and a parallel running period to ensure continuity. daappa Studio+ components can be added on top of Core or layered above a third-party fund accounting system where Core is not required.
Can we start with one component and add more later?
Yes. Most administrators start with daappa Core for fund accounting and add Studio+ components -- typically the Investor Portal and Extractor AI -- as their operations develop. Studio+ components are modular. You can add NAV Oversight, Analytics, or Compliance Monitoring independently. They connect to Core directly where both products are running.
Do you support Luxembourg fund structures?
Yes. daappa has active fund administrator clients in Luxembourg and a local French-speaking team. We support SIFs, RAIFs, SICAVs, SCSps, and SCA structures with CSSF regulatory reporting including BCL and AIFMD Annex IV. The platform is aligned with CSSF Circular 24/856 for NAV oversight obligations. Luxembourg is one of our core growth markets.
How does the Investor Portal work for our clients?
The Portal is deployed per fund manager client, branded with their identity. Your client's investors log in to their own branded environment -- they don't see daappa at all. You control document distribution, access rights, and what each investor can see. Capital account statements, fund documents, and performance reports are distributed directly from Core and DataHub into the Portal automatically.
What does implementation typically look like?
Every implementation is scoped individually based on your fund mix, client base, and current system. A straightforward boutique administrator implementing Core typically goes live in 10 to 16 weeks. More complex implementations with extensive historical data migration, multiple fund types, and Studio+ components take longer. We scope the timeline in the discovery phase before any commitment.
Is daappa suitable for an administrator being set up from scratch?
Yes. Several daappa clients have built their administration business on daappa from day one. Starting on daappa rather than migrating to it is easier -- no legacy data migration and no old processes to replace. The platform is designed to scale with your client base from 5 funds to 200+, so you are not buying something you will outgrow quickly.
